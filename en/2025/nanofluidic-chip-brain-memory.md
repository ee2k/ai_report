Scientists create nanofluidic chip with 'brain-like' memory pathways
by Monash University

Scientists at Monash University have created a tiny fluid based chip that behaves like neural pathways of the brain, potentially opening the door to a new generation of computers.

A coin sized chip made from a metal organic framework (MOF) can channel ions through microscopic pathways, acting like a transistor.

Unlike conventional chips, it also retains memory of past signals, imitating the plasticity of brain neurons, meaning it can both compute and learn, similar to a biological neural network.
